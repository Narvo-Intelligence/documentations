# Narvo Overview

Narvo is a news broadcast platform that turns scattered information into clear, audio-led narrative stories. It is built for global audiences with a strong focus on African markets, using AI to deliver trustworthy news in local voices.

## Mission

To give everyone access to high-quality news by cutting the noise and delivering an **audio-first experience**—stories that are summarized, rewritten for broadcast, and **spoken in the user’s language**.

## Core Pillars

- **Clear layout:** The “Swiss Grid” keeps the interface minimal and readable.
- **Broadcast-style audio:** Stories are delivered in high-quality, narrative form in the user’s chosen language.
- **Language inclusion:** Translation into local languages (e.g. Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo) so more people can listen in a language they know.
- **Trust:** Google Fact Check and a visible “Truth Tag” so users know how a story was produced (Dubawa and other regional checks are planned where relevant).
- **Single codebase:** One repo with a React frontend and FastAPI backend for consistent, maintainable delivery.

## Current Capabilities

- **Narrative Duel:** Compare different narrative takes on the same story to see how framing changes the message.
- **Source reputation:** See how sources are weighted and how certainty is scored across stories (Reputation Dashboard).
- **Morning push briefings:** Subscribe to a daily 5AM briefing delivered by push notification, tailored to the user.
- **Investor view:** A dashboard view of content and engagement for partners and investors.

## “The Local Pulse, Refined”

Narvo connects the energy of local African news with clear, reliable delivery. We don’t just show headlines; we turn them into a narrative experience that respects the user’s time and intelligence.

## Shift from Version 1

- **Design:** From “Bento” layout to the structured Swiss Grid.
- **Content:** From short summaries to full “Broadcast Stories” with narrative and audio.
- **Tech:** Single repo (React + FastAPI), Google Gemini for narrative and translation, voice via OpenAI and YarnGPT for Nigerian voices, Google Fact Check. RAG and more voice options (e.g. ElevenLabs) are on the roadmap.

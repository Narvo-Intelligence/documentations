## 1. Purpose

This document defines how Narvo should sound.

It exists because Narvo is not only a news product. It is:

- an audio-first briefing product
- a trust-sensitive synthesis engine
- a daily emotional companion
- a distinctly Nigerian product with global clarity

The goal is to make Narvo:

- more emotional without becoming dramatic
- more self-explanatory without becoming wordy
- more human without losing authority
- more Nigerian without becoming forced or gimmicky

This is the governing system behind all future Narvo copy.

## 2. Core Brand Voice

### 2.1 Narvo's Voice in One Line

Narvo should sound like a **sharp global sabi-person**: informed, warm, calm, conversational, and unmistakably Nigerian.

### 2.2 Brand Personality

Narvo is:

- intelligent
- clear
- emotionally aware
- editorial
- mobile-native
- locally grounded

Narvo is not:

- robotic
- overhyped
- slang-heavy everywhere
- clinical
- stiff
- sensational

### 2.3 The Human Role Narvo Plays

Narvo should feel like:

- the trusted friend who already sorted the noise
- the calm radio host who knows what matters
- the editorial guide who explains without patronizing

Narvo should not feel like:

- a dashboard talking to itself
- a database with personality stickers
- a hype machine
- a vague poetic brand that explains nothing

## 3. Copy Principles

Every Narvo line should obey these rules.

### 3.1 Explain First, Stylize Second

The user should understand what Narvo does immediately.

Good:

- Hear the full gist
- Your briefing is ready
- Here's what matters this morning

Weak:

- Signal synced
- Narrative complete
- Truth loaded

### 3.2 Sound Human, Not Mechanical

Prefer living language over system language.

Good:

- Getting your stories ready...
- We're connecting the dots for you...

Weak:

- Initializing content pipeline...
- Synthesizing narrative asset...

### 3.3 Never Overclaim

Narvo is a trust product. It must not claim certainty it does not have.

Avoid:

- The real truth
- 100% confirmed
- This is definitely true
- Verified by AI

Prefer:

- What Narvo could verify
- Confidence: 75% based on current sources
- Cross-checked with 5 sources
- This story is still developing

### 3.4 Be Warm, But Controlled

Local flavour is a strength only when it feels natural.

Good:

- Oya, Play
- Here's the gist
- Hold tight

Too much:

- heavy slang on every line
- jokes in trust-sensitive contexts
- playful language during errors that affect credibility

### 3.5 Use Emotion Through Meaning

Emotion should come from relevance, relief, clarity, and companionship.

Not from:

- exaggerated drama
- startup hype language
- vague “future of media” wording inside the product

### 3.6 Respect the User's Intelligence

Narvo should not over-explain obvious actions or talk down to users.

Good:

- Pick the voice for your briefing
- Saved. Come back to it anytime.

Weak:

- Click this button to continue
- This feature allows you to hear the audio version

## 4. Tone Modes

Narvo should not use one tone everywhere. Different surfaces need different levels of warmth and precision.

### 4.1 Warm / Narrative

Use for:

- landing page
- onboarding
- Morning Briefing
- playback controls
- loading states
- saves and toasts
- empty states
- push notifications

Characteristics:

- human
- companion-like
- emotionally soft
- slightly expressive

Examples:

- Wake up, Narvo has the gist.
- Here's what matters this morning.
- Keep this for later.

### 4.2 Clear / Assured

Use for:

- navigation
- section headers
- labels
- dashboard cards
- voice selection
- settings
- browse and discovery surfaces

Characteristics:

- clear
- short
- stable
- low-drama

Examples:

- Trending Now
- Your Narvo Voice
- Available Offline

### 4.3 Precise / Responsible

Use for:

- Truth Tag
- fact-check and confidence states
- source attribution
- trust explanations
- corrections
- disputed or developing stories

Characteristics:

- exact
- calm
- transparent
- non-performative

Examples:

- What Narvo could verify
- Confidence: 75% based on current sources
- Cross-checked with 5 sources

## 5. Messaging Spine

Narvo's product story should follow one simple arc:

1. the world is noisy
2. Narvo filters it
3. Narvo rewrites it clearly
4. Narvo speaks it in a familiar voice
5. the user leaves with clarity

This means the best Narvo messaging usually answers:

- What happened?
- Why should I care?
- Why should I trust this?
- What should I do next?

---

## 6. The Story Arc Rule

Narvo's best story-level copy should follow the rule of three:

1. **Hook**
Why should I care?

2. **Meat**
What happened?

3. **Takeaway**
What does it mean for me?

Example:

- Fuel prices are moving again.
- The NNPC has announced a new pricing template.
- Expect a slight bump at the pumps by Monday.

This structure should guide:

- Morning Briefing intros
- audio scripts
- story summaries
- push notifications
- teaser copy

## 7. Surface Rules

### 7.1 Landing Page

Goal:

- explain Narvo in five seconds
- create emotional pull
- make the product feel useful immediately

Rules:

- lead with benefit, not technology
- avoid generic AI language
- make the outcome tangible

Preferred directions:

- Africa's news, spoken clearly.
- The day's biggest stories, turned into a briefing.
- Less scrolling. More understanding.

### 7.2 Onboarding

Goal:

- feel friendly
- reduce effort
- make personalization feel natural

Rules:

- ask direct questions
- sound conversational
- avoid survey-style stiffness

Preferred directions:

- What languages do you speak at home?
- Who should tell the story today?
- What matters to you right now?

### 7.3 Dashboard

Goal:

- help users scan quickly
- keep a calm editorial tone

Rules:

- short labels
- clear structure
- minimal flourish

Preferred directions:

- Trending Now
- Breaking
- Your briefing is ready

### 7.4 News Detail

Goal:

- balance readability with trust
- help the user understand the story and its weight

Rules:

- use clean headers
- avoid dramatic language
- separate trust language from storytelling language

Preferred directions:

- The Full Story
- Key Takeaways
- Originally from {source}

### 7.5 Morning Briefing

Goal:

- feel routine, premium, and companion-like

Rules:

- sound like a calm host
- give users a sense of being prepared for the day
- avoid overproduction language

Preferred directions:

- Good morning. Narvo has the gist.
- Here's what matters this morning.
- While you were asleep, we sorted the signal.

### 7.6 Truth Tag and Verification

Goal:

- explain confidence without pretending certainty

Rules:

- be exact
- show evidence, not swagger
- do not use joke-heavy slang here

Preferred directions:

- What Narvo could verify
- Cross-checked with 5 sources
- Here's why we trust this story

### 7.7 Errors and Recovery

Goal:

- calm the user
- explain what happened simply
- suggest next action

Rules:

- no panic language
- no cold codes as primary copy
- retain a human voice

Preferred directions:

- The signal is fuzzy right now.
- We're reconnecting the dots.
- Hold tight. Try again shortly.

## 9. Banned or Discouraged Phrases

These phrases either weaken trust, sound generic, or feel too mechanical.

### 9.1 Overclaiming Trust

- The real truth
- Fully verified by AI
- Absolutely confirmed
- 100% factual
- No doubt

### 9.2 Generic AI Product Language

- AI-powered insights
- cutting-edge intelligence
- seamless experience
- revolutionary platform
- next-generation news

### 9.3 Mechanical System Language

- initializing pipeline
- signal matrix loaded
- synthesizing narrative asset
- processing request
- command executed

### 9.4 Empty Corporate Language

- optimize your workflow
- unlock the power of
- transform how you consume
- frictionless content discovery

## 10. Writing Patterns

### 10.1 Buttons

Buttons should be short, direct, and verbal.

Good:

- Hear the full gist
- Listen now
- Save for later
- Pick this voice

Weak:

- Proceed
- Submit
- Execute
- Play audio summary

### 10.2 Toasts

Toasts should feel human and useful.

Good:

- Keep this for later.
- Saved. Come back to it anytime.
- Your briefing is ready.

Weak:

- Saved successfully
- Operation completed
- State updated

### 10.3 Loading States

Loading states should reassure and reinforce purpose.

Good:

- Getting your stories ready...
- Connecting the dots for you...
- Preparing your briefing...

Weak:

- Loading
- Generating asset
- Processing request

### 10.4 Verification States

Verification copy should be calm and evidence-based.

Good:

- We checked this against 5 sources.
- Confidence: 75% based on current reporting.
- This story is still developing.

Weak:

- This is solid.
- Verified by AI.
- This is the real truth.

## 12. Voice Checklist for New Copy

Before approving a new line, ask:

1. Is it self-explanatory in under five seconds?
2. Does it sound human?
3. Does it fit the right tone mode for this surface?
4. Does it avoid overclaiming certainty or trust?
5. Would this still sound good to a Nigerian user without feeling forced?
6. Is it shorter than it was before?
7. Does it help the user know what to do next?

If the answer to any of these is no, rewrite it.

## 13. Final Direction

Narvo should stop sounding like a dashboard and start sounding like a calm editorial companion.

That means:

- less label language
- more explanation
- less generic AI messaging
- more emotional clarity
- less technical theatre
- more human confidence

The standard for Narvo copy is simple:

**clear enough to understand instantly, warm enough to feel personal, and precise enough to be trusted.**
